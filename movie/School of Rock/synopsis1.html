Synopsis:

School of Rock is a 2003 comedy film starring Jack Black as Dewey Finn, a down-on-his-luck musician who is kicked out of his band. Desperate for money, Dewey poses as his roommate, a substitute teacher, and takes a job at a prestigious private school.

Although he has no teaching qualifications, Dewey soon discovers that his class of straight-A students are musically talented. He forms a secret rock band with them, aiming to win a local Battle of the Bands competition. Along the way, Dewey teaches the kids about rock music, self-expression, and teamwork.

The film explores themes of individuality, breaking free from societal expectations, and the transformative power of music. The kids gain confidence and Dewey learns responsibility, creating an uplifting and humorous journey for both the students and the unconventional teacher.
